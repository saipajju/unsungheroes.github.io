Khudiram Bose's Early Life and Inspirations

Khudiram Bose was from a small village called Mohobani which was in West Bengal's Midnapore. Bose was born on December 3, 1889. Many say that Khudiram’s legacy has been largely limited to Bengal.


Growing up, he faced several devastations. He lost his mother at the tender age of 6, and his father just a year later. After which, Bose encountered revolutionaries like Barindra Kumar Ghosh of Calcutta. Khudiram Bose showed a revolutionary spirit even when he was a mere child. As a child Khudiram Bose loved adventure and was widely known for his courage in the face of danger. It was in the years 1902 - 1903 that Khudiram Bose was inspired to plunge into an active freedom struggle. During this time Sri Aurobindo and Sister Nivedita were in Medinipur to deliver a lecture inspiring people to join the freedom struggle against the British during the Swadeshi Movement, which highly inspired Khudiram.
